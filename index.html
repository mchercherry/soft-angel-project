Grassroots

SAP's Conception

SAP (Soft Angel Project) was officially founded in 1941, but the discovery of cannies can be traced back to 1919. An unidentifiable creature was found miles away from a military base, with a nest spread across a 10 mile radius. While probing the suspected nest yielded no identification nor further signs of life, this first creature (affectionately called an angel by an on-sight private) revealed a world wide phenomena several countries were beginning to endure. Dubbed "cannies" for their human-like qualities, our program seeks to solve the Great Canny Mystery.

Louis Garnier

Doctor Garnier was one of the most passionate researchers we had ever come across. His contributions to canny research are innumerable. His theories on the American canny population is still a major guideline for the project, and all future theories are derived in his thinking. Garnier sought out to emphasize the human qualities of cannies. Beyond surface physical observations, Garnier had mapped out organ structures, mind maps, and successfully ran experiments observing canny social skills and
social needs.

Bug Theory

In the same vein that hoverflies disguise themselves as bees, Doctor Garnier proposed the theory that cannies had evolved human-like traits to protect themselves from unknown predators. American canny ages were found to be remarkably old; canny ancestors may have existed all the way back to the Paleolithic era. Perhaps then these old predators have gone extinct. Their evolved traits would make them an invasive species in our society, but Garnier hoped that with rehabilitation they could be successfully and safely integrated amongst the American population.

VERNIKORSAK

Venus Theory

After Garnier had passed away, Doctor Josef Koch (current principal researcher) took charge with his own variation on
Bug Theory. Gearing experimentation and studies away from commonalities shared between humans and cannies, Koch sought out to define in clear terms what separates us and what their roll in the food chain would be. While cannies in America could yield many benefits, Koch has pushed the program towards analyzing if they are safe to even release back into the wild after the program is completed.

Joseph Koch giving orientation.

Program Expansion

Doctor Koch has successfully pushed this program into a limitless study; gearing government attention further towards the importance of canny studies, he has nearly tripled government funding for more intense and in-depth experimentation. Staff size has also grown, allowing for more collaboration and specialized rolls. Koch has made moves towards expanding this program internationally, but it is unlikely other countries would cooperate with the study. Please keep all information confidential, more than your job will be on the line.

Staff Safety

Doctor Koch has made a number of changes from Garnier's era, limiting canny enrichment measures for the sake of staff. Cannies should not interact with each other, and staff should not speak with them, make eye-contact with them, or approach them without protective gear. Group-experienced side-effects have been nearly eliminated, and multiple fail-safes have been implemented to ensure staff is not harmed in research. Orientation will go over protective measures and good practices to keep yourself and your team safe.
